Our vision and goals

DWP is beginning an ambitious period of modernisation over the next 3 years. This work will focus on the 3 key departmental priorities that will make the biggest difference to people who need to use and work with DWP’s services in the years ahead:

  • maximising employment and in-work progression
  • improving people’s quality of life
  • delivering excellent services for citizens, colleagues and taxpayers

As a department we will start by:

  • focusing on our great people by developing a flexible, inclusive, continuously-learning department
  • delivering joined-up modern services tailored to customer needs
  • enabling better outcomes by concentrating on the difference we make to customers’ lives, supported by data

We are concentrating on the areas where we can have the biggest benefits on outcomes for people who need to use or work with DWP's services.

We believe there are 4 main goals to meet so that we can achieve this:

  1. Simplify DWP's customer and colleague experiences, so people get the support they need faster.
  2. Build a consistent DWP experience, so people get the same level of service regardless of which part of DWP it comes from.
  3. Understand DWP's users and their personal situations by joining up data and insight, so we can tailor their experience.
  4. Design for inclusion, so everyone can access the support they need.

Our guiding principles

We will follow 8 principles in our work to provide efficient, safe and inclusive access to a suite of common services, products and capabilities:

  • user-centricity: enabling DWP to support customers according to their needs
  • simplification: so customers can access support faster
  • consistency: so customers can achieve the same level of service across DWP
  • data and insight: making data-driven decisions to improve the experience
  • experimentation: testing often, learning fast
  • collaboration: bringing the service lines together
  • culture: building and evolving brilliant teams that have fun
  • reusability: shared data, shared events, shared across services
